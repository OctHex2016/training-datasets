<noinput>
14. Output: 
The Monty Hall problem is a probability puzzle based on a TV game show. There are three doors, behind one of which is a prize, and behind the other two are goats. The contestant chooses a door, then the host, who knows where the prize is, opens one of the other two doors to reveal a goat. The contestant is then given the option to switch their choice to the remaining unopened door, or stick with their original choice. It's counterintuitive, but the optimal strategy is to switch doors because you now have a 2/3 chance of winning the prize.
