A rainbow
15. Output: 
Rainbows are formed due to the refraction, dispersion, and reflection of sunlight within raindrops. When sunlight passes through raindrops, the light is refracted, or bent, then dispersed into its individual colors because each color travels at different speeds. The light then reflects off the back of the raindrop and refracts again as it exits the raindrop, forming the arc of the rainbow. The order of colors in a rainbow from the outer edge is red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet.
