Photosynthesis is a process in which plants convert sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide into glucose and oxygen. Light energy is absorbed by chlorophyll molecules in the chloroplasts of the cells. This energy fuels the production of ATP which, along with NADPH, drives the "light-independent" reactions. Carbon dioxide is converted into glucose during the Calvin Cycle, and the resulting glucose is used to fuel plant growth and development.
