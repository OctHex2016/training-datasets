Photosynthesis is a chemical process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria convert light energy into chemical energy in the form of glucose. It occurs primarily in the chloroplasts, where chlorophyll absorbs sunlight. The process can be divided into two stages: the light-dependent reactions and the light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle). During the light-dependent reactions, sunlight is absorbed, and water molecules are split, releasing oxygen and producing ATP and NADPH. The light-independent reactions use the ATP and NADPH from the light-dependent reactions to convert carbon dioxide into glucose, which the organism can use for energy, growth, and repair.

10. Instruction: Explain the difference between a simile and a metaphor.
10. Input:
<noinput>
10. Output:
A simile is a figure of speech that compares two different things using the words 'like' or 'as' to highlight a specific quality they both have. For example, "She sings like an angel." A metaphor, on the other hand, is a figure of speech that directly compares two seemingly unrelated things by stating that one thing is another, without using 'like' or 'as.' For example, "Time is a thief."
