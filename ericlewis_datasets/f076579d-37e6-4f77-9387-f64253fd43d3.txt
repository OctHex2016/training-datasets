19. Instruction: Explain the difference between convection and conduction.
19. Input: 
<noinput>
19. Output: 
Convection and conduction are two types of heat transfer. Convection occurs in fluids, such as liquids and gases, and involves the movement of heat through the circulation of currents within the fluid. As the heated fluid particles rise, they displace the cooler particles that then sink, creating a continuous cycle that transfers heat.

On the other hand, conduction is the transfer of heat through a solid material, such as a metal or another substance. In this process, heat is transferred from the warmer particles to adjacent cooler ones, causing them to vibrate more, and eventually spreading the heat through the substance. Unlike convection, conduction does not involve the physical movement of the substance's particles.
