15. Instruction: Describe the process of photosynthesis.
15. Input:
<noinput>
15. Output:
Photosynthesis is the process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria convert sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water into glucose (a sugar) and oxygen. The process has two main stages: the light-dependent reactions and the light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle). 

During the light-dependent reactions, which occur in the thylakoid membranes of the chloroplasts, sunlight is absorbed by pigments like chlorophyll. This energy is used to produce ATP (adenosine triphosphate), a molecule that stores and transports energy, and NADPH, an electron carrier. Oxygen is released as a byproduct through the splitting of water molecules.

In the light-independent reactions or Calvin cycle, the ATP and NADPH produced in the light-dependent stage are used to convert carbon dioxide into glucose. This chemical process, which takes place in the stroma of chloroplasts, involves a series of enzyme-catalyzed reactions known as carbon fixation, reduction, and regeneration.
