The French Revolution lasted from 1789 to 1799 and unfolded in several distinct phases:

1. Economic Crisis: France faced severe financial issues, starving population, and increased public unrest in the late 1700s, leading to calls for change.

2. Establishment of the National Assembly: In 1789, the Estates-General was convened by King Louis XVI to address the financial crisis but could not agree on reforms. The representatives of the third estate broke away and formed the National Assembly.

3. Fall of the Bastille: On July 14, 1789, the people of Paris stormed the Bastille prison, symbolizing the end of the monarchy's power.

4. Declaration of the Rights of Man: In August 1789, the National Assembly adopted the Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen, asserting universal rights and the sovereignty of the people.

5. Radicalization: In 1793, King Louis XVI was executed, France became a republic, and the Reign of Terror ensued, resulting in tens of thousands of executions.

6. Aftermath: In 1799, Napoleon Bonaparte seized power in a coup and ruled as emperor, marking the end of the French Revolution. The Revolution left lasting impact on French society and politics, and inspired future revolutionary movements around the world.
