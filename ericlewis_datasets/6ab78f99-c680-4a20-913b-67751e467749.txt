13. Instruction: Describe the structure of the formal debate.
13. Input: <noinput>
13. Output:
A formal debate includes the following components:

1. Introduction: The debate opens with a brief explanation of the topic, format, and rules.
2. Opening Statements: Both sides deliver prepared speeches presenting the main arguments in favor of or against the motion.
3. Rebuttals/Cross-examinations: Each side challenges the arguments of the opposing side, asking questions, pointing out flaws or inconsistencies, and offering counterarguments.
4. Audience Questions: The audience has the opportunity to pose questions to address specific concerns or clarify issues.
5. Closing Statements/Summaries: Each side delivers a final speech summarizing their stance and responses to the rebuttals and questions, providing a last opportunity to persuade the audience.
6. Vote or Decision: The audience or assigned judge(s) determines a winner based on the quality and persuasiveness of the presented arguments.
