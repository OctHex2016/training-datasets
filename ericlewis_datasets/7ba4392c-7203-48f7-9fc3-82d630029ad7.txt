17. Output:
Photosynthesis is the process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria convert sunlight, carbon dioxide (CO2), and water (H2O) into glucose (sugar/energy) and oxygen (O2). This process occurs in chloroplasts, which contain the green pigment chlorophyll. During photosynthesis, the absorbed light energy excites electrons in chlorophyll, leading to a series of chemical reactions called light-dependent reactions. These reactions produce energy molecules (ATP and NADPH), which are then used in light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle) to convert CO2 into glucose, releasing O2 as a byproduct.
