Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide into energy-rich glucose and oxygen. This process takes place inside the chlorophyll-containing cells called chloroplasts, found within a plant's leaves. The process consists of two main stages: the light-dependent reactions and the light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle). In the light-dependent reactions, sunlight is absorbed by pigments such as chlorophyll, and water molecules are split to release oxygen and produce energy carriers (ATP and NADPH). Next, during the light-independent reactions, energy carriers and carbon dioxide are used to produce glucose through a series of chemical reactions known as the Calvin cycle. Earthquakes and hurricanes are both natural disasters that can cause significant damage and loss of life. The main difference between them is their origin and mechanism.
