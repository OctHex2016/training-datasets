20. Instruction: Describe the process of photosynthesis.
20. Input: 
<noinput>
20. Output: 
Photosynthesis is a process in plants, algae, and some bacteria, where they convert light energy from the sun into chemical energy stored as glucose or other organic molecules. It occurs in chloroplasts, which contain green pigments called chlorophyll that absorb light. Photosynthesis consists of two stages: the light-dependent reactions and the Calvin cycle (light-independent reactions). In the light-dependent reactions, light is absorbed, water molecules are split, and oxygen gas is released. Simultaneously, energy is stored in the form of ATP and NADPH. The Calvin cycle uses that energy, along with carbon dioxide, to synthesize glucose through a series of enzyme-mediated reactions, which can be used by the organisms for metabolic activities or stored for future use.
