19. Instruction: Write a brief explanation of how the Doppler effect works in relation to sound waves.
19. Input: <noinput>
19. Output: 
The Doppler effect occurs when a sound wave's frequency changes due to the relative motion between the source of the sound and the observer. As the sound source approaches the observer, the sound waves become compressed, resulting in a higher frequency (or pitch) perceived by the observer. Conversely, when the sound source moves away from the observer, the sound waves become less compressed, leading to a lower frequency (or pitch). This phenomenon is commonly experienced when a moving vehicle, such as a car or train, passes by and the sound it produces seems to change in pitch.
