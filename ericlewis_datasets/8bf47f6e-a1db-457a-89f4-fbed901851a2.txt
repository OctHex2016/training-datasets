10. Instruction: Explain how the process of photosynthesis works in plants.
10. Input: 
<noinput>
10. Output: 
Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert light energy into chemical energy to fuel their growth. It mainly occurs in the chlorophyll-containing cells of the leaves, specifically in the chloroplasts. The process consists of two main stages: the light-dependent reactions, which take place in the thylakoid membranes, and the light-independent reactions, which occur in the stroma. During the light-dependent stage, light energy is absorbed and converted into ATP and NADPH, and water molecules are split, releasing oxygen gas as a byproduct. The ATP and NADPH generated in this stage provide energy for the light-independent stage, also known as the Calvin cycle. In the Calvin cycle, carbon dioxide is converted into glucose through a series of enzyme-catalyzed reactions. The glucose produced can then be used by the plant for growth, maintenance, and reproduction.

12. Instruction: Explain how a refrigerator works.
12. Input: 
<noinput>
12. Output: 
A refrigerator works by utilizing the principles of heat transfer and the vapor-compression refrigeration cycle to remove heat from the refrigerated space and evacuate it to the surrounding environment. A refrigeration system generally consists of a compressor, condenser, expansion valve, and an evaporator. The refrigerant, a substance with a low boiling point, circulates through these components. The compressor increases the pressure and temperature of the refrigerant vapors, and the condenser cools the hot vapors, causing them to condense into a liquid. This high-pressure liquid refrigerant then passes through the expansion valve, which releases some pressure, and it becomes a mixture of liquid and vapor. Finally, the evaporator absorbs the heat from the refrigerator's interior, causing the refrigerant to vaporize and cool the inside of the fridge. The vapors then return to the compressor, and the cycle repeats.
