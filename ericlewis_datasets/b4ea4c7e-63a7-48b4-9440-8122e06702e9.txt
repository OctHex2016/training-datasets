9. Instruction: Describe the process of photosynthesis in plants.
9. Input:
<noinput>
9. Output:
Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert light energy into chemical energy. It occurs in chloroplasts and involves two stages: the light-dependent reactions and the light-independent reactions (Calvin Cycle). During the light-dependent reactions, plants absorb sunlight, which triggers the production of ATP and NADPH. During the light-independent reactions, CO2 is fixed into glucose using the generated ATP and NADPH, providing energy for the plant's growth and maintenance.
