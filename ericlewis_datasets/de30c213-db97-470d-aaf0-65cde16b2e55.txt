17. Instruction: Define the term "precipitation" and explain its different forms.
17. Input:
<noinput>
17. Output:
Precipitation refers to the process of water falling from the atmosphere to the ground, in the form of rain, snow, sleet, or hail. Rain occurs when water droplets in clouds combine and become too heavy, falling as liquid. Snow forms when water vapor in clouds freezes, creating ice crystals that fall as individual snowflakes. Sleet is a mix of rain and snow, where raindrops become ice pellets upon hitting the cold ground. Hail consists of frozen raindrops or ice balls pushed upward by strong winds, accumulating layers of ice before falling to the ground.

20. Instruction: Tell me how to plant and care for a tomato plant.
20. Input:
<noinput>
20. Output:
1. Choose a sunny location with well-drained soil or use a large container with quality potting soil.
2. Plant seedlings 2-4 weeks after the last frost, burying them up to 2/3 of their height to encourage strong roots.
3. Space the plants 24-36 inches apart to allow air circulation and sunlight penetration.
4. Water consistently, providing 1-2 inches of water per week, focusing on soil and avoiding leaves to prevent diseases.
5. Apply a layer of mulch to preserve moisture, regulate soil temperature, and prevent weeds.
6. Stake or cage your tomato plants to support their growth and keep fruit off the ground.
7. Regularly check for pests or diseases and treat accordingly.
8. Fertilize based on the specific needs of your soil and plant variety, typically after initial fruit set.
9. Harvest when the fruit is firm and fully colored, enjoying your delicious, homegrown tomatoes.
