7. Instruction: Explain the main differences between aerobic and anaerobic exercise.
7. Input:

7. Output: 
Aerobic exercise involves activities that require oxygen to be circulated to your muscles to produce energy. Some examples of aerobic exercises are walking, jogging, swimming, and cycling. These activities are generally moderate-intensity and can be performed for extended periods.

Anaerobic exercise, on the other hand, involves short bursts of high-intensity activity where your body demands energy more quickly than oxygen supply allows. Examples of anaerobic exercises include weightlifting, sprinting, and high-intensity interval training. During anaerobic exercise, the body primarily relies on glycogen and adenosine triphosphate (ATP) as energy sources, rather than oxygen.

In summary, aerobic exercise involves lower-intensity, longer-duration activities that rely on oxygen for energy production, while anaerobic exercise includes higher-intensity, shorter-duration activities that primarily use non-oxygen dependent energy sources.
