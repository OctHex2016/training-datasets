7. Instruction: Describe how photosynthesis works in plants. 
7. Input: <noinput>
7. Output: 
Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert light energy into chemical energy to fuel their growth. In the presence of sunlight, plants absorb carbon dioxide from the atmosphere and water from soil through their leaves and roots, respectively. Within the chlorophyll-containing chloroplasts in the plant cells, light energy is used to break down water molecules into oxygen (O2) and hydrogen (H). The oxygen is released as a byproduct while the hydrogen combines with carbon dioxide to form glucose. This glucose is then converted into other organic compounds and energy stores, which are essential for the plant's growth and maintenance.
