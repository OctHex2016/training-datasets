6. Input:

6. Output: 
Vector graphics utilize mathematical equations and geometric shapes (lines, curves, points, polygons) to create images. These graphics are resolution-independent, meaning they can be scaled to any size without losing quality and remain crisp and smooth at any zoom level.

Raster graphics (or bitmap graphics) represent an image by encoding a grid of individual pixels, with each pixel assigned a specific color or shade. When zoomed in or enlarged, raster graphics can lose quality, appearing pixelated, because they are resolution-dependent.

In summary, vector graphics excel in scalability, maintaining image quality at any size, while raster graphics are better for detailed images and complex color gradients but can lose quality when resized or zoomed in on.
