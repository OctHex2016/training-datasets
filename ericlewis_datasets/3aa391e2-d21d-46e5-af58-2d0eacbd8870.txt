2. Timing: Plant tomatoes after the last frost date when the soil temperature reaches at least 60°F.
3. Location and soil: Plant tomatoes in a sunny spot with well-draining, slightly acidic soil. Add compost to improve soil fertility.
4. Planting: Dig a hole about 1ft deep, place tomato seedling with 1/3 of stem buried in the ground. Space plants about 2-3 feet apart.
5. Water and fertilize: Keep soil consistently moist but not soggy; use tomato-specific fertilizer or organic compost.
6. Support: Stake or cage tomato plants for better growth and fruit quality.
7. Prune: Remove suckers and yellowing leaves to promote air circulation and prevent diseases.
8. Monitor pests and diseases: Check for tomato hornworms, aphids, and signs of fungal infections, take action promptly.
