13. Instruction: Compare and contrast aerobic and anaerobic exercise.
13. Input: <noinput>
13. Output:
Aerobic exercise involves continuous and rhythmic movements, engaging large muscle groups and increasing heart rate over prolonged periods, improving cardiovascular fitness. Examples include walking, jogging, and swimming. In contrast, anaerobic exercise relies on short bursts of intense activity, utilizing stored energy and muscle strength without sufficient oxygen, forming lactic acid. Examples include lifting weights, sprinting, and high-intensity interval training (HIIT). Aerobic exercises increase endurance while anaerobic exercises primarily build muscle strength and power.
