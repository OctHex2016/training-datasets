19. Input:
<noinput>
19. Output:
The placebo effect plays a crucial role in clinical trials as it helps account for the subjective aspects of medical interventions. When participants receive a placebo treatment that they believe is a real medication, they may feel better or report improvements – this psychological response can skew the trial's results. By comparing the effects of the treatment group with those of a placebo-controlled group, researchers can assess the true efficacy of the intervention while minimizing the bias caused by participants' expectations, thus ensuring the trial produces valid and reliable results.
