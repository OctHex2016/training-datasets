10. Instruction: Explain the difference between weather and climate.
10. Input: <noinput>

Weather refers to the short-term atmospheric conditions in a specific area, such as temperature, humidity, precipitation, and wind, which can change frequently throughout the day or week. Climate, on the other hand, is the long-term average pattern of weather conditions in a particular region, typically calculated using data from a 30-year period or longer. In simpler terms, weather is what we experience on a day-to-day basis, while climate reflects the average conditions we can expect over extended periods of time.
