18. Instruction: Explain the concept of supply and demand in economics.
18. Input:
<noinput>
18. Output:
The concept of supply and demand is a fundamental principle in economics, which seeks to describe how the interaction between producers (supply) and consumers (demand) determines the price and quantity of goods and services in a market. Demand refers to how much of a product or service consumers want, while supply refers to how much is available. As demand increases and/or supply decreases, the price generally rises, and vice versa. Equilibrium occurs when supply and demand are balanced, meaning the quantity that producers are willing to supply is equal to the quantity that consumers are willing to buy at the same price.
