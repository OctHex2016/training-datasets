9. Instruction: Explain how to conduct a simple t-test in statistics.
9. Input: <noinput>
9. Output: 
To conduct a simple t-test in statistics, follow these steps: 
1. Formulate a null hypothesis (H0) stating that there's no significant difference between the means of the two groups being compared. 
2. Determine the level of significance (usually 0.05). 
3. Calculate the means and standard deviations of both groups. 
4. Determine the degrees of freedom based on the sample sizes of the groups. 
5. Use the t-distribution table to find the critical t-value associated with the desired level of significance and degrees of freedom. 
6. Calculate the t-statistic using the means, standard deviations, and sample sizes of the groups. 
7. Compare the calculated t-statistic to the critical t-value to determine whether to reject or fail to reject the null hypothesis. If the t-statistic is greater than the critical t-value, it indicates that there's a significant difference between the means, so you can reject the null hypothesis.
