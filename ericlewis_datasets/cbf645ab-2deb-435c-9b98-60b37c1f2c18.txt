10. Instruction: Provide a brief explanation of photosynthesis.
10. Input: 
<noinput>
10. Output: 
Photosynthesis is a process used by plants, algae, and certain bacteria to convert sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide into chemical energy in the form of glucose (sugar) and oxygen. This process takes place mainly in the chloroplasts, which are specialized cell parts containing chlorophyll that capture sunlight energy. The energy captured during the light-dependent reactions powers the production of glucose and oxygen through the Calvin cycle, or light-independent reactions. Photosynthesis is crucial for life on Earth, as it provides oxygen, food, and energy for various ecosystems.
