The Olympic Games originated in ancient Greece around 776 BC as a religious and athletic festival honoring Zeus. The ancient games, held in Olympia every four years, included events such as footraces, wrestling, and chariot racing. The ancient Olympic Games eventually declined and were abolished in 393 AD by Emperor Theodosius I. The modern Olympic Games, inspired by the ancient games, were revived by Frenchman Baron Pierre de Coubertin, who founded the International Olympic Committee (IOC) in 1894. The first modern Olympic Games were held in Athens in 1896, with 14 nations participating. The modern Games now include the Summer Olympics, the Winter Olympics, and the Paralympics, with thousands of athletes from around the world competing every four years.
