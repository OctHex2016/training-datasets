7. Instruction: How does photosynthesis work, and what are its main products?

7. Input: 
<noinput>
7. Output: 
Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants and some other organisms convert sunlight into chemical energy in the form of glucose. The process takes place in the chloroplasts, the organelles containing chlorophyll, a pigment that absorbs light energy. Photosynthesis consists of two stages: the light-dependent reactions and the light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle). During the light-dependent reactions, sunlight is absorbed, and energy is used to split water molecules (H2O) into oxygen (O2), which is released, and hydrogen ions (H+). The light-independent reactions then use the hydrogen ions, along with carbon dioxide (CO2), to produce glucose (C6H12O6) through the Calvin cycle. The main products of photosynthesis are glucose, which provides energy for the plant, and oxygen, which is released into the atmosphere.
