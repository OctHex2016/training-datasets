19. Instruction: Write a recipe for a chocolate chip cookie.
19. Input: 
<noinput>
19. Output: 
Chocolate Chip Cookie Recipe:

Ingredients:
- 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
- 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
- 1 cup unsalted butter, room temperature
- 1/2 cup granulated sugar
- 1 cup packed light-brown sugar
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
- 2 large eggs
- 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips

Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C).
2. In a bowl, whisk together flour and baking soda; set aside.
3. In a separate bowl, beat butter, granulated sugar, light-brown sugar, salt, and vanilla until creamy.
4. Add eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition.
5. Gradually add the flour mixture to the wet ingredients and mix until combined.
6. Stir in chocolate chips.
7. Drop spoonfuls of dough onto baking sheets lined with parchment paper, 2 inches apart.
8. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until edges are golden brown.
9. Allow cookies to cool on baking sheets for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

8. Instruction: Compare and contrast renewable energy sources with non-renewable energy sources.

<noinput>
8. Output: 
Renewable energy sources are derived from natural resources that can be replenished over time, while non-renewable energy sources come from finite resources that get depleted over time. Renewable sources include solar, wind, hydro, geothermal, and biomass. Non-renewable sources are fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and natural gas, as well as nuclear power.

Renewable energy sources:
- Environmentally friendly with lower greenhouse gas emissions
- Sustainable long-term supply
- Potentially cheaper or more cost-effective over time
- Can reduce reliance on foreign energy sources

Non-renewable energy sources:
- Contribute to air and water pollution
- Limited availability as resources decline over time
- Prices can be volatile and subject to geopolitical tensions
- Greater risk of accidents, such as oil spills and nuclear disasters
