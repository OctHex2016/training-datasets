19. Instruction: Explain the process of photosynthesis in plants.
19. Input: 
<noinput>
19. Output: 
Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants and certain other organisms convert sunlight into chemical energy stored in glucose. It occurs mainly in the chlorophyll-containing cells of leaves. Photosynthesis consists of two stages: the light-dependent reactions and the light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle). In the light-dependent reactions, pigments (chlorophyll) absorb sunlight and water molecules are split, producing oxygen gas and high-energy molecules (ATP and NADPH). During the light-independent reactions, also known as the Calvin cycle, carbon dioxide is fixed into organic molecules using the energy provided by ATP and NADPH, ultimately forming glucose as the product.
