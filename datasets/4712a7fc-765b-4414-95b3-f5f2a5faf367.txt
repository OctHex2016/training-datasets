20. Instruction: Summarize the Fibonacci sequence, its origin, and its uses.
20. Input: 
<noinput>
20. Output: 
The Fibonacci sequence is a series of numbers in which each number is the sum of the two preceding ones, typically starting from 0 and 1 (0, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, ...). It was introduced by Leonardo Fibonacci in 1202, as a solution to a problem involving the growth of rabbit populations. The sequence is found in various natural phenomena, such as the arrangement of spirals in sunflower seeds or nautilus shells, and it has applications in art, architecture, and mathematics, including the golden ratio and fractals.
