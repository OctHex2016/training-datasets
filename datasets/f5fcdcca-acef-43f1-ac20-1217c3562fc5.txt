20. Instruction: Explain the difference between a migraine and a regular headache.
20. Input: 
<noinput>
20. Output: 
A migraine is a neurological condition characterized by intense, throbbing pain often on one side of the head, accompanied by symptoms like nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light and sound. Migraines typically last longer and can cause debilitating discomfort, often requiring rest in a dark, quiet room. On the other hand, a regular headache or tension headache usually presents as a dull, aching, and steady pain on both sides of the head, with few, if any, additional symptoms. Regular headaches are typically more manageable and shorter in duration compared to migraines.
