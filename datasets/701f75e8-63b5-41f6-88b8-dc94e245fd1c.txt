<noinput>
18. Output:
The Turing test, proposed by the mathematician and computer scientist Alan Turing, is a test for evaluating a machine's ability to exhibit human-like intelligence. It involves a human judge engaging in a natural language conversation with a machine and another human without knowing which is which. If the judge cannot consistently distinguish between the machine and the human based on their responses, the machine is considered to have passed the test, displaying artificial intelligence indistinguishable from human intelligence. The Turing test is significant because it sets a benchmark for assessing the advancement of AI and its capacity to mimic human-like thought processes and behavior.
