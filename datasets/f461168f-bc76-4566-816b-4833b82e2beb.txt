15. Instruction: Explain how photosynthesis works.
15. Input:
<noinput>
15. Output:
Photosynthesis is a process used by plants, algae, and some bacteria to convert light energy from the sun into chemical energy in the form of glucose or other sugars. In the presence of sunlight, carbon dioxide (CO2) from the atmosphere and water (H2O) from the roots are absorbed by chlorophyll-containing cells inside chloroplasts in the plant's leaves. The light-dependent reactions capture energy from the sun in the form of ATP and NADPH, while the light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle) use these energy carriers to fix CO2 into glucose. Oxygen (O2) is released as a byproduct of the process.
