19. Instruction: Explain how photosynthesis works in plants.
19. Input:
<noinput>
19. Output: 
Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert light energy into chemical energy to fuel their growth. It takes place in the chloroplasts, the green pigmented organelles inside plant cells, and involves two main stages: the light-dependent reactions and the light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle). During the light-dependent reactions, chlorophyll and other pigments absorb light and generate ATP (adenosine triphosphate) and NADPH (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate hydrogen), energy-rich molecules. In the Calvin cycle, these energy-rich molecules are used to convert carbon dioxide and water into glucose and other organic compounds. Oxygen is released as a byproduct during this process.
