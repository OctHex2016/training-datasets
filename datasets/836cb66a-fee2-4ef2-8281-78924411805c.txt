Ingredients:
- 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
- 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
- 1 cup unsalted butter, room temperature
- 1/2 cup granulated sugar
- 1 cup packed light-brown sugar
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
- 2 large eggs
- 2 cups semisweet and/or milk chocolate chips

Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Whisk together flour and baking soda in a bowl and set aside.
2. In a separate bowl, beat the butter, granulated sugar, brown sugar, salt, and vanilla until pale and fluffy. Add the eggs, one at a time, mixing well after each addition.

The greenhouse effect is a natural process in which greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide, methane, and water vapor, trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere. These gases allow shortwave sunlight to pass through the atmosphere and reach the Earth's surface.
