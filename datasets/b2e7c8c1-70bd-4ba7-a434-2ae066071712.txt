The main difference between renewable and non-renewable energy resources lies in their availability and replenishment. Renewable energy resources are naturally replenished and can be used indefinitely. They include solar, wind, hydropower, geothermal, and biomass energy. These resources are considered environmentally friendly and sustainable because they have a lower impact on the environment and can be harnessed indefinitely without depleting the Earth's resources.

On the other hand, non-renewable energy resources are finite and cannot be replenished in a human timescale. They are derived primarily from fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and natural gas. Non-renewable resources also include nuclear energy, which is derived from the decay of uranium. These resources are known to generate significant greenhouse gas emissions and contribute to air pollution, making them less environmentally friendly. Once non-renewable resources are depleted, they cannot be replaced, and this poses a risk to future energy availability.

A suitable exercise program for senior citizens could include the following activities:
1. Warm up: 5-10 minutes of light aerobic activities, like marching in place or arm swings, to increase heart rate and loosen joints.
2. Strength training: Perform 2-3 sets of 8-12 repetitions of exercises targeting the major muscle groups. Examples include seated leg lifts, standing bicep curls, overhead shoulder press using light hand weights, or resistance bands.

6. Cool down: Finish each session with 5-10 minutes of light stretching and deep breathing to promote relaxation and aid in recovery.
