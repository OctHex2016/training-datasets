Ingredients:
- 2 oz tequila
- 1 oz lime juice (freshly squeezed)
- 1 oz triple sec (Cointreau preferred)
- Salt for rimming the glass (optional)
- Lime wedge for garnish

Instructions:
1. Rub a lime wedge around the rim of a chilled margarita glass, then dip the rim in salt to coat it (optional).
2. In a cocktail shaker filled with ice, combine the tequila, lime juice, and triple sec.

20. Instruction: Briefly describe the Krebs cycle.
20. Input: 
<noinput>
20. Output: 
The Krebs cycle, also known as the citric acid cycle, is a series of chemical reactions that occur within the mitochondria of cells. These reactions generate ATP (adenosine triphosphate), the body's main source of energy, as well as NADH and FADH2, which are electron carriers. The cycle also produces carbon dioxide and water as waste products. The Krebs cycle plays a vital role in cellular respiration, helping to convert carbohydrates, fats, and proteins into usable energy.
