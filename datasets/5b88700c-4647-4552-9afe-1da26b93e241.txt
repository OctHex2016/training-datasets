11. Input: 
<noinput>

11. Output: 
Photosynthesis is the process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria convert sunlight into chemical energy. During photosynthesis, organisms capture light energy using pigments, primarily chlorophyll. Then, in a series of complex reactions, captured light energy is combined with carbon dioxide (CO2) and water (H2O) to produce glucose (C6H12O6), a sugar that serves as a primary energy source for the plant. Oxygen (O2) is produced as a byproduct of the reaction and is released into the atmosphere, providing vital oxygen for respiration in other organisms.

20. Instruction: Explain the greenhouse effect in simple terms.

20. Input:
<noinput>

20. Output: 
The greenhouse effect is a natural process that helps regulate Earth's temperature. Solar energy from the sun enters Earth's atmosphere, and some of this energy gets absorbed by the Earth's surface, warming it up. The surface then radiates some of the absorbed energy back into the atmosphere as heat. Certain gases, called greenhouse gases (such as carbon dioxide and methane), trap this heat energy within the Earth's atmosphere, preventing it from escaping into space. This trapped heat keeps our planet warm enough to sustain life. However, when the concentration of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere increases due to human activities, it leads to an enhanced greenhouse effect, resulting in global warming and climate change.
