Greenhouse gases (GHGs) are gases that trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, contributing to the greenhouse effect, which leads to global warming and climate change. These gases allow sunlight to enter the atmosphere but prevent heat from escaping, thereby increasing Earth's average temperature.

Ozone-depleting substances (ODS) are chemicals that cause the depletion of the ozone layer in the Earth's stratosphere. The ozone layer protects life on Earth from harmful ultraviolet (UV) radiation. Examples of ozone-depleting substances include chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) and hydrochlorofluorocarbons (HCFCs). ODS break down ozone molecules through a series of chemical reactions, reducing protection from UV radiation.

Both GHGs and ODS have a negative impact on the environment, but their effects are different. GHGs influence global temperatures and climate change, while ODS primarily affect the Earth's ozone layer and increase the risk of harmful UV radiation exposure.
