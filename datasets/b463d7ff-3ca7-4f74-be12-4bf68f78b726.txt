Fermentation in food production is a natural chemical process in which microorganisms, like bacteria, yeast, or fungi, convert carbohydrates, such as sugars, into other compounds, like alcohol or acids, under anaerobic (oxygen-free) conditions. Examples of fermented foods include yogurt, pickles, sauerkraut, and sourdough bread.
