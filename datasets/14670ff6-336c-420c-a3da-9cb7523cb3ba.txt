11. Input:
<noinput>
11. Output:
Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water into glucose (energy) and oxygen. It primarily takes place in the chlorophyll-containing chloroplasts of plant cells. The process consists of two main stages: the light-dependent reactions and the light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle). In the light-dependent reactions, sunlight is absorbed by chlorophyll, and water molecules are split to release oxygen and produce ATP (adenosine triphosphate) and NADPH (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate). The ATP and NADPH molecules are then used in the Calvin cycle to fix carbon dioxide into glucose, providing energy for the plant's growth and maintenance.

18. Instruction: Explain how an internal combustion engine works.
18. Input:
<noinput>
18. Output:
An internal combustion engine is a type of engine where fuel and air are mixed and burned inside the engine itself, typically in cylinders. The burning process converts the chemical energy in the fuel into mechanical energy, moving the pistons within the cylinders. This linear movement is then converted into rotary motion by the connecting rods and crankshaft, propelling the vehicle forward. The four-stroke internal combustion engine follows four main steps: intake, compression, combustion (also known as power stroke), and exhaust. These steps correspond to the intake of air-fuel mixture, compression of the mixture, ignition with a spark or heat, and expulsion of exhaust gases.
