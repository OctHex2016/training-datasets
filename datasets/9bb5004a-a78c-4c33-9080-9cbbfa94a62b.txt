11. Output: 
1. Ensure the scene is safe, and then approach the person.
2. Tap the person's shoulders and shout, "Are you OK?" to check responsiveness. If unresponsive, call for help or have someone call emergency services.
3. Position the person on their back on a firm, flat surface.
4. Kneel beside the person and place one hand on their forehead and the other under their chin. Tilt the head back and lift the chin to open the airway.
5. Check for signs of normal breathing for no more than 10 seconds. If not breathing or gasping, begin CPR.
6. Place the heel of one hand in the center of the person's chest, and the other hand on top, interlocking your fingers. Keep your arms straight and your shoulders above your hands.
7. Perform 30 chest compressions, pushing straight down about 2 inches and allowing the chest to recoil back up between compressions. The rate should be 100-120 compressions per minute.
8. After 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths by tilting the person's head back, pinching their nose closed, and breathing into their mouth until you see their chest rise. Each breath should take about 1 second.
9. Continue the cycle of 30 compressions and 2 breaths without pausing, until the person starts breathing, professional help arrives, or an AED is ready for use.
