17. Output: 
Mitosis is the process of cell division where one cell copies its genetic material (DNA) and divides into two identical daughter cells. It consists of several stages: prophase, metaphase, anaphase, and telophase. During these stages, the DNA first condenses into chromosomes, which then align in the center of the cell. The chromosomes are then pulled apart to opposite ends of the cell, and the cell finally splits into two new cells, each containing identical genetic information.
