12. Instruction: How does a digital signature work in cryptographic systems?
12. Input: 
<noinput>
12. Output: 
A digital signature is a cryptographic technique used for data authentication and integrity protection. It involves two key processes: signing and verifying. In the signing process, the sender uses their private key to create a unique signature for the data being sent. The signature depends on the data and the signing private key. In the verifying process, the recipient uses the sender's public key to check if the signature is valid. If the signature is valid, it ensures that the data hasn't been tampered with and confirms the sender's identity.
