20. Input:

20. Output: 
Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants and some other organisms convert sunlight into chemical energy. It occurs mainly in the chloroplasts of plant cells, using light energy to produce carbohydrates (glucose) from carbon dioxide and water. During this process, oxygen is released as a byproduct. Photosynthesis involves two main stages: the light-dependent reactions, which take place in the thylakoid membranes of chloroplasts and generate ATP (energy) and NADPH (electrons), and the light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle), which use the energy and electrons from the light-dependent reactions to fix carbon dioxide and synthesize glucose.
