18. Instruction: Can you prove me how Pythagorean theorem works?
18. Input: 
<noinput>
18. Output: 
In a right-angled triangle, the Pythagorean theorem states that the square of the length of the hypotenuse (the side opposite to the right angle) is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides. Mathematically, it is represented as a² + b² = c², where c is the hypotenuse, and a and b are the other two sides.

Proof: Consider a right-angled triangle with sides a, b, and c. With the same lengths, create a square with side c. This square's area is equal to c². Now, place four triangles inside the square so that their hypotenuses align with the sides of the square. These four triangles occupy the entire square. The remaining square areas inside the square are two smaller squares with sides a and b.

Thus, the area of the large square (which is equivalent to c²) can also be calculated as the area of the four triangular regions plus the areas of the two smaller squares, i.e., 4 * (1/2) * a * b + a² + b². Upon simplification with the triangle area: 2 * a * b + a² + b² = c². This equation demonstrates the validity of the Pythagorean theorem for any right-angled triangle.

19. Instruction: Can you explain what the Doppler effect is?
19. Input: 
<noinput>
19. Output: 
The Doppler effect, named after Christian Doppler, is a phenomenon that occurs when the frequency and wavelength of a wave, such as sound or light, change due to the relative motion between the source of the wave and the observer. When the source and observer move towards each other, the observed frequency increases and the wavelength shortens, resulting in a higher-pitched sound or a shift toward the blue end of the light spectrum. Conversely, when the source and observer move away from each other, the frequency decreases and the wavelength lengthens, yielding a lower-pitched sound or a shift toward the red end of the spectrum. This effect is commonly experienced when a car or train approaches and then passes by an observer, altering the sound's pitch as it moves.
