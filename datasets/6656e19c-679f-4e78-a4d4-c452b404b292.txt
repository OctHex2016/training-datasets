17. Instruction: Explain briefly how the human eye perceives color.
17. Input:
<noinput>
17. Output:
Color perception occurs when light enters the human eye and activates special cells called photoreceptors, specifically cones. Cones are found in the retina and come in three types, each most sensitive to a different wavelength of light: red (long wavelengths), green (medium wavelengths), or blue (short wavelengths). When a mixture of wavelengths from a light source enters the eye, the cones are activated in varying degrees, and the brain processes these signals to interpret the object's color.
