24. Instruction: Briefly describe the process of photosynthesis.
24. Input:
<noinput>
24. Output:
Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants, algae, and some bacteria convert sunlight, carbon dioxide (CO2), and water (H2O) into sugars and oxygen (O2). This process occurs primarily in the chloroplasts of plant cells, which contain the green pigment chlorophyll. Photosynthesis consists of two stages: the light-dependent reactions and the light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle). The light-dependent reactions occur when light energy is absorbed by chlorophyll, producing ATP and NADPH, which are then utilized in the light-independent reactions. In the Calvin cycle, CO2 is incorporated into organic molecules, eventually producing glucose and other sugars that the plant uses for growth, respiration, and reproduction.
