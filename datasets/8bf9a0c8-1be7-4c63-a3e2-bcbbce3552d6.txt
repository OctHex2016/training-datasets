For someone with lower back pain, the following exercise routine can be helpful:

1. Gentle stretching: Perform gentle stretches for the hamstrings, lower back, and hip flexors to help alleviate tension.
 
2. Pelvic tilts: Lie down on your back, bend your knees, and keep your feet flat on the ground. Slowly rock your pelvis back and forth, engaging your lower abdominal muscles and glutes.

3. Cat-Cow stretch: On your hands and knees, gently alternate between arching and rounding your back to improve spinal mobility.

4. Bird-Dog exercise: Start on all fours, then slowly raise and extend your opposite arm and leg while maintaining balance and stability.

5. Bridges: Lie on your back with your knees bent, feet flat on the ground, and arms by your side. Lift your hips off the ground, squeezing your glutes as you rise.

6. Walking or swimming: Low-impact aerobic activities such as walking or swimming can help with pain management and overall back strength.
