9. Output:
The Turing test, proposed by Alan Turing in 1950, is a test for determining a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior that is indistinguishable from that of a human. In the test, a human judge engages in a natural language conversation with a machine and a human separately. The test's significance lies in its attempt to define a benchmark for machine intelligence. If the judge cannot reliably distinguish between the machine and the human based on their responses, the machine is said to have passed the test, suggesting it can exhibit human-like intelligence.
