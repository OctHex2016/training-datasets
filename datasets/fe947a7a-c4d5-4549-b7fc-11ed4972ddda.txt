18. Input: 
<noinput>
18. Output: 
Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert light energy from the sun into chemical energy in the form of glucose. During photosynthesis, green plants absorb sunlight and carbon dioxide from the atmosphere and water from the soil through their roots. With the help of chlorophyll, which is present within chloroplasts in plant cells, and sunlight, they convert water and carbon dioxide into glucose and oxygen. The glucose produced is used by the plant for its energy needs, growth, and reproduction, while the oxygen is released back into the atmosphere.
