14. Input: <noinput>
14. Output:
The greenhouse effect is a natural process where certain gases in the Earth's atmosphere, such as water vapor, carbon dioxide, and methane, trap heat from the sun. These gases allow sunlight to pass through but retain the heat that gets radiated back from the Earth's surface, thus warming the planet. This effect is essential for maintaining temperatures suitable for life. However, human activities have led to increased greenhouse gas emissions, intensifying the greenhouse effect and causing global warming.###

18. Instruction: Compare and contrast the two art movements: Impressionism and Expressionism.
18. Input: <noinput>
18. Output:
Impressionism is an art movement that emerged in the 19th century, focusing on the effects of light and color in the natural environment. Impressionist artists often painted outdoors and aimed to capture fleeting moments and sensations. Key Impressionist artists include Monet, Renoir, and Degas.

Expressionism, on the other hand, is a 20th-century art movement, mainly characterized by the artist's subjective emotions and perspectives. Expressionist artists sought to evoke strong emotional reactions in the viewer, often using distortion and intense colors. Notable Expressionist artists include Edvard Munch, Wassily Kandinsky, and Egon Schiele.

In summary, Impressionism focuses on the sensory experience of the world, while Expressionism emphasizes personal emotions and responses.###
