19. Output: 
To make delicious chocolate chip cookies, follow these simple steps:

1. Preheat the oven to 375°F.
2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together 1/2 cup of butter, 1/4 cup of granulated sugar, and 1/2 cup of brown sugar until light and fluffy.
3. Beat in 1 egg and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract.
4. In a separate bowl, whisk together 1 1/4 cups of all-purpose flour, 1/2 teaspoon of baking soda, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt.
5. Gradually fold the dry ingredients into the wet mixture, mixing until just combined.
6. Stir in 1 cup of semi-sweet chocolate chips.
7. Drop spoonfuls of dough onto a cookie sheet, leaving 2 inches between each cookie.
8. Bake for 10-12 minutes, until the edges are lightly browned.
9. Allow the cookies to cool on the sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.
Enjoy your delicious homemade chocolate chip cookies!

20. Instruction: Explain the difference between a simile and a metaphor in literature.
20. Input: 
<noinput>
20. Output: 
Similes and metaphors are both figurative language techniques used in literature to create imagery and comparisons. A simile is a comparison between two things using "like" or "as," while a metaphor is a direct comparison between two things, often using the verb "to be." For example, "Her hair was as golden as the sun" is a simile, while "Her hair was the sun" is a metaphor. Both techniques are used to evoke powerful emotions and create a more vivid picture in the reader's mind.
