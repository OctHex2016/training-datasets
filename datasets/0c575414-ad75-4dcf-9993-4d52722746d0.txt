Plokkfiskur (Icelandic Fish Stew)

Ingredients:
- 600g of white fish (haddock or cod)
- 600g of potatoes
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 3 tbsp of butter
- 200ml of milk
- 2 tbsp of all-purpose flour
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Chopped parsley for garnish

Instructions:
1. Boil the fish in lightly salted water until cooked through. Drain and let it cool. Flake the fish, removing any bones.
2. Boil the potatoes in unsalted water until tender. Drain and let them cool, then cut them into small cubes.
3. In a pan, melt the butter and saut√© the onion until it's soft and translucent.
4. In a small bowl, mix the flour with a little milk to make a paste. Add the paste to the cooked onions and stir for about a minute.
5. Gradually add the rest of the milk to the onion mixture, stirring constantly to avoid lumps.
6. Gently mix in the flaked fish and cubed potatoes. Season with salt and pepper.
7. Cook the stew gently for about 10 minutes until heated through. Be careful not to let it boil.
8. Serve hot, garnished with chopped parsley.

15. Instruction: Explain the process of photosynthesis and why it's important for living organisms.
15. Input: 
<noinput>
15. Output: 
Photosynthesis is a process used by plants, algae, and some bacteria to convert sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide into glucose, which serves as a source of cellular energy, and oxygen as a by-product. This process occurs in the chloroplasts of plant cells, where chlorophyll, a green pigment, absorbs light energy. The energy drives a series of chemical reactions transforming carbon dioxide and water molecules into glucose and liberating oxygen molecules. Photosynthesis is essential to life on Earth, as it provides most of the oxygen required by living organisms and forms the base of the food chain in ecosystems, transforming solar energy into organic molecules that are used as fuel by other living beings.
