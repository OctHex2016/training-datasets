1. Park on a level surface, engage the parking brake, and turn on hazard lights.
2. Locate the spare tire, jack, and lug wrench, typically found in the trunk.
3. Place wheel chocks or heavy objects behind the wheels to prevent rolling.
4. Loosen the lug nuts on the flat tire by turning counterclockwise with the wrench (do not remove them yet).
5. Position the jack under the car's jacking point and slowly raise the car until the flat tire is off the ground.
6. Fully remove the loosened lug nuts and pull the flat tire off.
7. Align the holes of the spare tire with the wheel bolts and mount it.
8. Hand-tighten the lug nuts onto the wheel bolts, then use the wrench to partially tighten diagonally.
9. Lower the car back onto the ground and remove the jack.
10. Fully tighten the lug nuts using the wrench in a diagonal pattern, and place the flat tire, jack, and tools back in the trunk.
