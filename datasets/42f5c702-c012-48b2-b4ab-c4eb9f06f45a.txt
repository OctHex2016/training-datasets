12. Instruction: Explain the concept of the bystander effect.
12. Input: 
<noinput>
12. Output: 
The bystander effect is a social psychological phenomenon whereby individuals are less likely to help a victim in an emergency when there are other people present. The more witnesses there are, the less likely it is that any individual will intervene. This occurs due to the diffusion of responsibility, where each person assumes someone else will take action.
