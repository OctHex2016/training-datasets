Parallel computing is a type of computing architecture where multiple processors, or computing units, work simultaneously to solve a problem or complete a task more quickly and efficiently. Instead of processing the information sequentially, as in traditional computing, parallel computing divides the workload into smaller parts, which are then assigned to multiple processors. These processors execute their assigned tasks concurrently, and the results are combined upon completion. Parallel computing is widely used in high-performance computing, scientific simulations, data processing, and machine learning applications.
